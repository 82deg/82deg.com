Who I am

Built from design. Wired for product.

I started in graphic design, wandered through editorial, found my home in UX, and eventually crossed over into product. That journey — nearly two decades of it — is why I think differently to most PMs. I bring the designer’s instinct for human behaviour and the strategist’s obsession with outcome to the same table.

At Backbase, I lead the product direction of backbase.io, the developer-facing knowledge hub we’ve been turning into an AI-first platform. Over the past year and a half I’ve been building the systems behind the scenes: knowledge retrieval, AI-driven findability, and the operational infrastructure that makes AI products reliable rather than just impressive. Real work, not demos.

I started 82 Degrees back in 2009. Originally a design studio, now the home for selected freelance AI product engagements and the side projects I build because I can’t help myself.

This is how I work

I help teams build AI that actually ships.

  • 01

    Direction & discovery

    Translate customer insight and business strategy into a prioritised AI product roadmap. Sharp theses on where AI actually earns its keep, tested against real users and honest evals — not wishful thinking.

  • 02

    Team rhythm & delivery

    Lead cross-functional, globally distributed product teams. Set the operating rhythm across design, engineering, data, and QA — so shipping AI stops being a heroic act and starts being a cadence.

  • 03

    Outcomes, not outputs

    Take features from prompt to production with guardrails, evals, and the signals that actually tell you it's working — so AI stops demoing well and starts delivering.
