About

From pixels to products to prompts.

I founded 82 Degrees back in 2009 as a design studio. Since then I’ve sampled most corners of the craft — graphic design, editorial, UX, digital strategy, and for the past decade, product leadership in fintech at scale.

Today I work as an AI-first Product Manager. That means I start from what the model can plausibly do, pair it with honest user research, and ship something with guardrails, evals, and a clear path to measurable impact — not a demo that dies in a slide deck.

Stanford Online Product Management (XPROD110, 2024). Currently Principal UX/UI Consultant at Backbase while taking on selected freelance AI product engagements under 82 Degrees.

What I do

Three ways I help teams ship AI.

  • 01

    AI product strategy

    Map where AI actually earns its keep in your product. Prioritise use cases by feasibility, value, and risk — then turn the shortlist into a concrete roadmap your team can execute against.

  • 02

    Discovery & prototyping

    Rapid prototyping with LLMs, evals, and real users in the loop. We learn fast which flows hold up under messy input and which are theatre — before you commit engineering quarters to them.

  • 03

    Shipping & measuring

    Taking features from prompt to production: guardrails, observability, evals, and the KPIs that matter. Boring, rigorous, compounding — exactly what AI features need to stop being demos.
